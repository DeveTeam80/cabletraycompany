Common Misconceptions About Cable Trays Debunked






15-February-2023 || By: Admin

In industrial environments, cables and wires are supported and protected by cable trays, which are a common component. Despite their widespread use and track record of success, there are a few misconceptions regarding cable trays that might cause misunderstandings and confusion. We will dispel some of the most widespread myths concerning cable trays in this blog.

Only heavy industrial settings employ cable trays.
One of the most widespread misconceptions regarding cable trays is that they are primarily utilized in high-intensity industrial environments like chemical or oil refineries. However, cable trays are adaptable and can be used in a variety of settings, including hospitals, universities, and commercial buildings as well as data centers. When electrical wires and wiring need to be supported and protected, cable trays are the perfect answer.

The cost of cable trays is excessively high.
Cable trays may cost more up front than other cable management options like conduit or wire mesh, but they are less expensive in the long run because they need less installation time, upkeep, and danger of cable breakage. In the long term, cable trays are a cost-effective option because they outlast other cable management techniques.

Installing cable trays is tricky.
Another misunderstanding regarding cable trays is that they are challenging to set up. Yet, cable trays can be made to fit any purpose and are actually quite simple to install. A broad variety of accessories are available to help personalize the installation to meet particular requirements, and the majority of cable trays come with pre-punched holes that make installation a snap.

The security of cable trays is inferior to conduit.
Conduit is viewed by some as a more secure cable management option than cable trays. Yet, cable trays are at least as secure as conduit. In order to keep electrical cables and wiring safe from harm or interference, cable trays are designed to support and protect them. Also, compared to conduit, cable trays provide better ventilation and heat dissipation, which may aid in extending the life of the wires.

Only brand-new buildings use cable trays.
Although cable trays are frequently utilized in new construction projects, they also make an excellent retrofit for older structures. Cable trays are a cost-efficient and useful option for restoration projects since they can be simply put in existing structures without the need for significant alterations.

Cable trays do not look attractive.
Some individuals think that cable trays are unattractive and can harm a building's aesthetic appeal. But, cable trays are a customized and aesthetically pleasing choice because they come in a wide range of materials, textures, and colors. Cable trays can be made to match the architecture and interior decor of the building, making them a discrete but efficient option.

Cable trays are a flexible, adaptable, and scalable choice for cable management in a range of applications. By busting these popular beliefs, we hope to encourage a better understanding of the benefits of cable trays and how they support and protect wiring and electrical cables. If you're considering using cable trays for your forthcoming project, be sure to talk with a reputable manufacturer and supplier to ensure you obtain the finest choice for your particular needs.
