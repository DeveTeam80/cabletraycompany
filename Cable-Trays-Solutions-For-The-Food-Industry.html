Cable Trays Solutions For The Food Industry







5-May-2022 || By: Admin



As a food company, we comprehend the part of the industry that is in charge of the food chain's procedures. The notion encompasses the steps of transportation, receipt, storage, processing, and conservation.

This industry's raw resources are primarily derived from plants (agricultural), animals (livestock), and fungi (related to mushrooms).



The advancement of this industry has been aided by advances in food science and technology. The rise in food production has been accompanied by a steady growth in the monitoring and assurance of hygiene and food regulations in countries that are attempting to control and harmonize processes and products.



Hutaib Electrical has been able to comprehend these new industry trends in order to provide our customers with a more competent service.



Our solutions

1. Clean and processing areas
The processing areas' facilities must be constructed in a hygienic manner to ensure food safety and eliminate the danger of contamination of any kind. Cleaning and disinfection should also be made easier, as they contribute to the installation's preservation and upkeep.

The right materials in the cable trays ensure that the installation can be thoroughly cleaned and that the surfaces resist contact with food and cleaning agents.

It should be noted that difficult-to-clean facilities will necessitate more intensive methods, more aggressive chemicals, or longer cleaning cycles, all of which will result in a greater cost.



2. Extreme temperatura zones
Extreme temperature zones are areas with extremely low temperatures, places where ambient temperatures can drop below - 20 oC and stay there for long periods of time. Very low temperature zones include freezing chambers and chilly rooms. It is important to note that the insulation and other protective features of the electrical equipment used in these places must not deteriorate at the temperature of operation.



The

cable trays in India

must be able to endure the pressures that arise from being exposed to the elements. As a result, when different areas of an internal channeling are exposed to extremely varied temperatures, such as in cold stores, the cable trays must be able to endure air circulation from the hottest to the coldest region of the channeling without losing their qualities.



3. Underground storage areas
Wet sites, such as those where floors, ceilings, and walls are or may be saturated with humidity and where muck, or thick drops of water owing to condensation, may emerge or be covered with mold for lengthy periods of time, are accessible underground storage facilities.
It is suggested that electrical material, particularly cable management systems, comply with IP protection when used for terminals, splices, and connections, as well as systems or devices that present the degree of protection corresponding to vertical drop and water spray in these locations.



4. Outdoor storage areas
Outdoor storage locations have a high incidence of climatic variables, such as dampness and sunlight, which cause rapid wear on any placed goods.

The usage of cable trays in outdoor installations will be defined by a minimum corrosion resistance of Class 5, with a galvanize specifically intended to withstand adverse weather for a long time.



5. Production control areas
The production control area is a room next to or distinct from the plant where all production activities are coordinated. All signals from the machinery arrive in this chamber, where they are processed to create the daily timetable that moves all of the factory's production activities.

Cable trays will be used indoors with little exposure to external elements that could shorten the product's lifespan.



INSTALLED PRODUCTS WITH RAW MATERIALS
The surfaces of the materials must be durable, cleanable, and, if necessary, disinfectable, without breaks, and resistant to cracking, chipping, scaling, or abrasion, preventing undesired materials from penetrating.



In addition, the following items will be provided:

  • Corrosion protection
  • Non-toxic
  • Non-absorbent
  • It should be free of odors, colors, and stains.



    • Materials in non-food areas should be made of corrosion resistant materials or materials that have been treated (coated or painted) to be corrosion resistant, and these surfaces should also be cleanable.



    CLEANING
