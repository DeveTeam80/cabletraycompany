    GI Lintels are commonly used in various construction applications, including:
  • Residential construction: Supporting doorways and windows in houses and apartments
  • Commercial buildings: Supporting openings in office buildings, retail stores, and other commercial structures.
  • Renovation projects:Replacing old or damaged lintels during renovations
  • Load-bearing walls: Supporting the weight of walls above openings in load-bearing structures.

    • GI Lintels come in various designs to suit different load requirements and opening sizes. Some common types include:
  • Single Skin Lintels: These are basic U- or C-shaped profiles used for lighter loads.
  • Channel Lintels: Made from rolled steel channels, offering higher load capacity
  • Built-up Lintels: Fabricated from multiple sections of GI sheet for customized strength requirements.
  • Lintels with Thermal Break: Include insulating material to improve thermal performance and reduce energy consumption.

  • Opening size: The lintel's size should be sufficient to support the weight of the wall above the opening.
  • Load requirements: Consider the weight the lintel needs to support, including the wall and any additional loads.
  • Wall cavity size:Choose a lintel with a profile that fits within the wall cavity.
  • Fire resistance rating:If fire resistance is a concern, select a lintel with an appropriate fire rating.
  • Engineered wood lintels: Can be a good option for some applications but may require additional fireproofing measures.
  • Steel lintels:Provide superior strength but are generally more expensive than GI lintels.
