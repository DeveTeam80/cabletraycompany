What is Cable Tray


A cable tray system is used in the electrical wiring of buildings to assist protect the electrical cables that are used for power transmission, control, and communication. Cable trays are frequently used for cable management in commercial and modern construction and are used as an alternative to opening wiring or electrical course constructions. They are especially useful when a wire framework is likely to undergo alterations since new cables can be added by laying them in the tray rather than putting them through a line.

Types of Cable Trays

- Hutaib Electricals:

Ladder Cable Tray :
Two side rails on the ladder cable tray are joined by rungs. This kind of cable tray works well because you can easily reach the cables from either the top or bottom thanks to the ladder rungs.
When the locations of the cables in horizontal cable tray runs must be maintained or in non-horizontal cable tray runs, the rungs of the ladder cable trays serve as ideal anchors for tying down the cables.

Perforated Cable Tray :
The Perforated Cable Trays allow for air flow, water drainage, and dust fall thanks to openings in the bottom of the trays. For small cable entry and exit, the ventilation opening's slots and holes can be used.

Wire Mesh Cable Tray :
A type of cable tray built of stainless steel wires by joining them with welding is known as a wire mesh cable tray, also known as a basket cable tray.
Low voltage, telephone, and fiber optic cables supported on short spans are the principal uses for cable trays.

Channel Cable Tray :
A type of fully enclosed cable tray constructed of stainless steel, carbon steel, FRP, and aluminum alloy is called a channel cable tray. Computer cables, communication cables, thermocouple cables, and other control cables are typically supported by channel cable trays with powder-coated, hot-galvanized, and electro galvanized surfaces. The enclosed cover on the channel type cable tray shields the cables from sunlight, flying debris, dust, and water or other liquids.

Raceways Cable Tray :
A raceway is an enclosed conduit that creates a physical channel for electrical wires (also known as a raceway system). Raceways shield wires and cables from physical dangers such as heat, humidity, corrosion, water penetration, and general abrasion. Another term for "surface mounted wire molding" is "raceway."
