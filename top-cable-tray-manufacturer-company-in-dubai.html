Cable trays come in various types and designs, each suited for different applications. Below are some of the most common types of cable trays:

Ladder Cable Tray:
This is the most popular type of cable tray. It is designed with two side rails connected by rungs, forming a ladder-like structure. The rungs can be spaced according to the cable diameter and the required load capacity.

Perforated Cable Tray


This type of cable tray features a series of holes on the tray's bottom and sides. The perforations allow for proper ventilation and drainage, making it ideal for outdoor applications.

Wire Mesh Cable Tray:
This type of cable tray is made of wire mesh that forms a basket-like structure. It is lightweight and flexible, making it ideal for applications that require frequent cable changes.

Solid Bottom Cable Tray:
This type of cable tray features a solid bottom that provides complete cable protection. It is suitable for applications where cable protection is critical, such as in chemical plants.



Using cable trays in your electrical system offers several benefits, including:

  1. Cable Protection:
    Cable trays protect cables from damage caused by moisture, dust, and debris.
  2. Easy Cable Management:
    Cable trays provide an efficient way to organize cables, making it easier to identify and manage them.
  3. Cost-Effective:
    Cable trays are cost-effective, as they reduce the need for cable supports, conduit, and other accessories.
  4. Easy Maintenance:
    Cable trays are easy to clean and maintain, ensuring a long service life
