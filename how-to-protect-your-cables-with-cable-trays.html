How to protect your cables with Cable Trays


You need to plan for your cable management whether you're working with your data center server cabinet, industrial generator parts, or manufacturing any units. These factors and the function of various solutions will be discussed in this article to make sure that your cable management system remains a benefit for many years to come, and if you are looking for a convenient and cost-effective option to keep your cables safe for many years, then cable trays are the choice which most of the industries are embrace for their own purpose.

It is crucial to know that different types of cables needs different treatment to make them secure for years:
  • If you manage electrical lines, the risk of overheating and even fire increases. Other potential consequences include cable breakage, insulation damage, crosstalk, and more seriously, fire.
  • Always use the correct length of cables otherwise it will be a mess for you, or make them properly fit into the cable trays so they will not get destroyed either harm you workers or environment.


Things to consider while

installing cable trays:



  • Don't overfill: Never overload a cable tray when installing; otherwise, it may catch fire or begin to cause short circuits. Always attempt to fill it to capacity.
  • Space between your trays: Another crucial consideration is to never forget to provide space between your wires and the cable tray when putting it in a high-rise structure. It simply means to leave some space between your trays so you can remove any damaged or useless cables with ease.
  • Choose cable trays accordingly: Wire stretching during installation must be avoided at all costs. Never cause a problem by choosing a tray that is the same width and length as your wires, there are different types of cable trays available at Hutaib Electricals for your various cable needs.
  • Routing it correctly: The proper routing of your cables is a crucial factor that you cannot ignore when installing a cable tray. This is due to the fact that if you provide it the wrong route, it could quickly become destroyed.


General advice to secure your cables:

  • Cable bend radius: Never bend the cable more than its radius otherwise you are not able to get the full productivity from the cables and also it will make you spend again and again as they spoil because of over bending.
  • Protecting your pass-through: On occasion, you'll need to drill holes in furniture, server containers, or other objects to make room for wires. It's crucial to utilize grommets when running wires through plastic, wood, or metal since these materials sometimes have sharp edges that could cut the cable or harm its insulation. As the cable travels through this opening, grommets shield it.
  • Overtighten your cables: Never tie your cables too tight or the ties will stop the signal traveling properly through your cables, it can also damage your cables and increase your expanse.


Here are the different types of cable trays which you can use:

Basket cable trays: They are referred to as go-to options for routing and supporting many cable runs in basket cable trays. The wired frame improves cable ventilation and air flow. This can be done using hanging tray systems, elevated floors, or underneath desks.

Perforated cable trays:

In order to lay power and signal cables for distributing electricity and signaling in industrial facilities, department stores, gyms, hospitals, airports, and other businesses, perforated cable trays are a type of cable tray with perforations on the bottom sheet and side rails.

Ladder cable trays: A ladder cable tray system is intended to be used as a support system for power cables or control cables. It is composed of two longitudinal side members joined by separate transverse elements.
