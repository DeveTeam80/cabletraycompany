HOW ARE STRUT CHANNELS USED?

Strut Cable Tray Support Channels

provide light structural support designed for use in construction and electrical industries. Commonly they are made of steel and are used to provide support for electric wiring and plumbing. Additionally, they can also be used to support mechanical components for larger appliances.

Cable Tray

Channel support systems are usually put together using U-shaped slotted steel channels that are integrated with fittings. This creates rigid metal structures that are strong enough to support electrical conduits, cables, panel boxes and other pieces of equipment with ease.


They can be utilized to create a permanent structure that is used to support wiring around a location or they can be used for temporary purposes to store different types of machinery and wires for temporary projects. Strut channels are an incredibly versatile support product that can be used in various applications. Different types of channel fasteners and fixings that are incorporated with these structures makes it a complete fixture solution for wiring support and handling.


Major Components Of A Strut Channel

The number of fasteners, bolts, screws, nuts, and brackets available will help make the strut channel system a very versatile and lightweight frame. However, to get the most out of the

Strut Channel Support System

, it is important to understand the various product-related terms and all available options. For this reason, we will discuss the individual components, types, sizes, and usages of the available accessories.


STRUTS

The main component of the strut channel is the strut itself. This is the name of the U-shaped slotted steel channel that acts as a component of the structural frame. With slots, you can attach a variety of fittings, brackets and hardware. There are also different channel styles in different shapes and sizes that can be combined to suit your exact needs.


FITTINGS

To connect strut parts to each other, it is important to select the appropriate hardware to support the mating of the parts. This improves structural integrity. There are various fittings that can expand the possibilities of lightweight frames, depending on how the strut channels are installed and assembled. Types of bracket fittings include small right-angle brackets, straight brackets, and flat corner brackets that help connect the two strut pieces. There are also various washers that help ensure the stability and integrity of the ground path to add support and reduce vibration and noise. Additional accessories such as nuts and bolts are also an integral part of the strut channel cable tray support system.
